The Problem
For Businesses

20–30% of digital ad spend is lost to fraud and invalid traffic (NEXD, 2025), with additional waste from poor targeting and ad-blockers. No direct access to verified customer data. Middlemen extract margin. Impressions paid for, actions not guaranteed.

The Solution
What is Deebak?

Deebak connects businesses with verified users through reward-based micro-tasks. Users complete actions — surveys, app installs, store visits, data collection — and earn credits redeemable as cash, crypto, or coupons. Businesses pay only per verified completion. Zero waste.

Credit & Payout System
Currency & Pricing

Tasks priced initially in ILS (₪). USD and USDT (stablecoin) to follow. Verified users can transfer credits peer-to-peer within the platform once their wallet is unlocked.

Cash-out Channels

Local wallets (Jawwal Pay, CliQ), bank transfers. Crypto (USDT) as an alternative channel. Minimum withdrawal threshold defined during pilot phase.

Frozen Credits Mechanic

All earned credits are locked until KYC and personal info verification is complete. If a user remains inactive for 6+ months with unverified or unclaimed credits, those balances transfer to Deebak's central wallet — creating a passive revenue stream while incentivizing active verification and participation.

08 · Product
Verification Logic
Deebak's anti-fraud and task verification system — the trust engine that makes the CPA model credible and valuable to business clients.
Verification Layers
1
Layer 1 — Identity
KYC & Profile Verification
Phone OTP, national ID scan, selfie liveness check, personal data verification. Required before any reward can be withdrawn. Prevents fake account farms and bot signups. High-value users (complete profiles) get access to better-paying tasks.
2
Layer 2 — Device
Device Fingerprinting & App Permissions
One account per device enforced. App requires camera, location, and notification permissions as a condition of use. Device ID, OS version, and app signature tracked. Rooted or emulated devices are flagged and blocked automatically.
3
Layer 3 — Behavioral
Task-Level Proof Collection
Per task type: surveys use time-on-screen + response pattern analysis; installs use deeplink + app open event; store visits use GPS geofence + on-site QR scan; data collection uses image AI validation; social actions use OAuth token verification.
4
Layer 4 — Analytics
Pattern & Anomaly Detection
ML-based anomaly detection on completion velocity, location clustering, reward withdrawal patterns. Users completing tasks too fast, from the same GPS coordinates as others, or with identical response patterns are automatically flagged for review.
5
Layer 5 — Human Review
Admin Escalation Queue
Flagged completions route to the Super Admin review queue. Human reviewer approves or rejects. Repeated violations result in account suspension and credit forfeiture. Businesses are notified of any completions that fail final review.

Storage & Security Architecture
ID Document Storage

National ID scans and selfies are stored encrypted at rest (AES-256) in isolated cloud storage (AWS S3 with server-side encryption). Access is restricted to the KYC review queue only. Documents are deleted from active storage after KYC approval — only a verified status flag is retained in the main database. Raw ID images are never shared with business clients under any circumstance.

Personal Profile Data

Income, profession, and demographic data are stored pseudonymously — linked to an internal user token, not to name or national ID. Business clients receive only aggregated, anonymized audience insights from campaign analytics. No individual profile is ever exposed to a business client. Data is processed on servers hosted within the EU or GCC jurisdiction where applicable.

Regulatory Compliance Strategy
Palestine — Primary Jurisdiction

Palestine does not yet have a comprehensive data protection law equivalent to GDPR, but the Palestinian Monetary Authority (PMA) governs digital financial services. Deebak will register as a fintech operator with PMA and operate under its Electronic Payment and Settlement Systems Regulation. User consent for data collection will be explicit, granular, and revocable — modeled on GDPR best practices voluntarily, ahead of expected Palestinian data protection legislation.

Crypto Redemption — Legal Position

USDT (Tether) redemption is offered as a non-custodial transfer — Deebak facilitates the transfer but does not hold crypto on behalf of users. This positions the service as a payment facilitator rather than a crypto exchange, reducing regulatory exposure. Users in jurisdictions where crypto is restricted may be limited to fiat cash-out options only. Legal counsel will confirm jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction eligibility before each market launch.

Conflict Context — West Bank Operations

The West Bank operates under a complex legal framework (Oslo Accords) that restricts telecommunications spectrum and requires coordination with Israeli authorities for 4G deployment. As of January 2026, Israel authorised 4G enhancement for West Bank Palestinians — a significant positive for Deebak's mobile infrastructure. Gaza operations are suspended indefinitely due to ongoing conflict and infrastructure destruction. All initial operations are West Bank-focused, with Gaza re-entry planned only after full infrastructure restoration.

Fact-Check: Figures & Statistics
⚠ MENA Ad Market — "$10B+ in 2019" — Outdated & Understated

The document cites "$10B+ spent on digital ads in MENA in 2019" sourced to Statista. This figure is stale and misleading by 2026 standards. IAB MENA's 2024 study confirms MENA digital ad spend reached $6.25 billion in 2023 (digital only), while total advertising across all channels exceeded $8–12 billion. Using a 2019 figure in a 2026 investor document signals a lack of current research. The pitch should use the 2023/2024 IAB MENA figure and note the 13.6% year-on-year growth trajectory.

⚠ Ad Waste "40%+" — Imprecise & Needs Source

The "40%+ media waste" claim is cited without a source. Research data shows ad fraud accounts for roughly 12–23% of digital ad spend globally (Juniper Research, ANA 2024). The broader "waste" figure — including poor targeting, viewability issues, and MFA sites — can reach 20–30% by some estimates (NEXD, 2025). The "40%+" claim is defensible only if combining fraud, poor targeting, and ad-block losses together, but this must be explicitly sourced. Unsourced round numbers undermine credibility with sophisticated investors.

✓ "450+ ads per day" — Directionally Correct

Various studies estimate daily ad exposure between 4,000–10,000 impressions for heavy users, but "seen" or "noticed" ads are estimated at 300–700. The "450+" figure is within defensible range for conscious ad encounters in MENA markets with high mobile usage. Acceptable, but should be attributed to the specific Global Web Index 2020 study cited in the pitch deck.

✓ "3.5h+ daily screentime" — Confirmed

Confirmed by multiple sources. Astute Analytica (2024) reports MENA users spend an average of 3.5 hours daily on social media platforms alone — consistent with the document's claim. This figure is accurate and well-supported.

⚠ Global Gig Economy "$500B+" — Correct but Needs Update

The "$500B+ global gig economy" (Statista 2025) is now confirmed accurate — multiple 2024–2025 sources put the figure at $461–582 billion, so "$500B+" is a reasonable approximation. However the document should note this is projected to grow to $582B in 2025 and $2.17 trillion by 2034 at 15.79% CAGR (Market Reports World, 2025) — the growth story is far more compelling than the current snapshot.

✗ Reward Apps Market "$15B+ at 10% CAGR" — Significantly Inaccurate

This is the most problematic figure in the document. Multiple 2024 research sources put the global cashback and rewards app market at $3.5–$8.5 billion in 2024 (not $15B), growing at 7–12% CAGR (not 10%). The $15B figure appears to conflate the broader loyalty program market ($100B+) with the specific app segment. This discrepancy is material — an investor who checks this will lose confidence. Recommend correcting to: "Rewards app market: ~$8B (2024), projected $23B by 2033 at 12.4% CAGR (Credence Research, 2025)."

⚠ MENA Youth Unemployment "~27%" — Overstated

The ILO's 2024 Global Employment Trends confirms North Africa youth unemployment at 22.3–22.5%, and the World Bank's MENA developing countries figure for 2024 is 19.6%. The "~27%" cited likely comes from an older or differently scoped dataset (possibly including Arab States with female unemployment weighted in). Should be updated to "~22% (ILO, 2024)" to avoid being challenged on sourcing.

⚠ Palestine Mobile Penetration "~72%" — Understated

DataReportal's Digital 2024 report shows Palestine had 82.2% mobile connection penetration and 88.6% internet penetration as of January 2024. PCBS data confirms 83% smartphone ownership in the West Bank specifically. The "~72%" in the market table is outdated — updating to ~82–88% strengthens the addressable market argument considerably.

⚠ "200M+ MENA Smartphone Users" — Needs Clarification

GSMA Intelligence projects unique mobile subscribers in MENA at 459 million by 2025. The "200M" figure likely refers to smartphone internet users specifically, not total mobile connections. The distinction matters — clarify the exact metric and source. The actual addressable base is larger than stated, which is a positive for the pitch.

First-mover in an overlooked market. No direct equivalent exists in Palestine or the immediate region. The local trust advantage is real and hard for outsiders to replicate quickly.

No pricing model is defined. What does a campaign cost? What's the minimum spend? These are the first questions a business client asks.
